# Sales Dashboard Analysis – Case Study

## 1. Background

Sales data is one of the most important parts of any business. It helps understand what is selling, what is not, and how overall performance is changing over time.

This project analyzes sales data to identify patterns and create a simple dashboard for business insights.

---

## 2. Problem

Businesses often struggle with questions like:

* Which products are driving most of the revenue?
* Which categories are underperforming?
* How do sales change over time?
* Where are the gaps in performance?

The goal of this project was to answer these questions using data.

---

## 3. Dataset

The dataset contains sales-related information such as:

* Product name
* Category
* Quantity sold
* Sales amount
* Date of sale
* Customer information (if available)

Some cleaning was needed before analysis.

---

## 4. Approach

### Data cleaning

* Removed missing and duplicate values
* Fixed inconsistent formats
* Standardized product and category names

### Analysis

Focused on:

* Total sales by product and category
* Monthly or time-based trends
* Top-performing products
* Low-performing products

### Dashboard

A simple dashboard was created to visualize:

* Sales KPIs
* Category performance
* Product-level insights
* Trend analysis

---

## 5. Findings

### Sales concentration

A small number of products are responsible for most of the total revenue.

---

### Category performance

Some categories consistently perform better than others, showing stable demand.

---

### Time-based trends

Sales are not constant and show fluctuations across different time periods.

---

### Underperforming products

Some products have consistently low sales, which may need review or removal.

---

## 6. Business insights

The data suggests that focusing on high-performing products and categories can significantly improve overall sales performance.

Also, tracking trends regularly can help in making better stock and marketing decisions.

---

## 7. Recommendations

* Focus marketing on high-performing products
* Review or discontinue low-performing items
* Monitor sales trends monthly
* Improve product mix based on category performance

---

## 8. Conclusion

This project shows how sales data can be turned into meaningful business insights using simple analysis and dashboards.

It also highlights the importance of focusing on a few key products that drive most of the revenue.

---

## 9. What I learned

* How to analyze sales performance
* How to build simple dashboards for reporting
* How to extract business insights from raw data
* How data supports better business decisions
